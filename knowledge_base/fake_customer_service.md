# Fake Customer Service / Telecom Impersonation

Category: 假冒客服詐騙

Description:
Scammers impersonate customer service agents from telecoms, e-commerce platforms, streaming services, or financial institutions. They claim there is a billing error, an unauthorized subscription, or an account issue requiring the victim's cooperation. Unlike the installment-cancellation scam, these messages focus on account access, subscription cancellation, or refund processing — and may ask for credentials rather than ATM operations.

Commonly impersonated services:
- Telecoms: 中華電信, 台灣大哥大, 遠傳, 亞太電信
- E-commerce: momo購物, 蝦皮, PChome, 博客來
- Streaming: Netflix, Disney+, Spotify
- Financial: Line Pay, 街口支付, 悠遊付

Key signals:
- unsolicited contact claiming an account error or unauthorized charge
- requests for account login credentials, OTP, or credit card verification
- offer of refund that requires "verification" first
- link to a fake customer service portal
- caller insists on staying on the line while victim completes steps
- legitimate-looking but slightly wrong domain (e.g., momo-service.xyz vs momo.com.tw)

Note — distinguishing legitimate billing notifications:
Safe billing messages typically: come from an official shortcode or verified LINE account, state the exact charge amount, do NOT ask for credentials or OTP, and direct to the company's known official website. Treat any billing message that asks for action beyond logging into the official site as suspicious.

Example messages:
> 您好，我是momo購物客服，您的帳號近期有異常訂單，請點擊 https://momo-service.xyz/verify 配合確認，否則訂單將自動成立並扣款。

> Netflix通知：您的帳號付款方式已過期，請在48小時內至 https://netflix-tw.xyz/billing 更新，否則服務將暫停。

> 您好，我是中華電信客服，系統偵測到您的帳號被申請了加值服務，需要您提供簡訊驗證碼才能取消，請問您現在方便嗎？
